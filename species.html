Our Trophy Species

Six premier African game species available for hunting at Goldcrest

Kudu

The greater kudu is one of Africa's most elegant antelope. Bulls stand up to 1.5 meters at the shoulder and carry magnificent spiraling horns that can exceed 60 inches. Known for their elusiveness and sharp senses, kudu offer a challenging and rewarding hunt in dense bushveld terrain. Their grey-brown coat with white vertical stripes provides exceptional camouflage, making spot-and-stalk hunting a true test of skill.

Blue Wildebeest

The blue wildebeest, or brindled gnu, is an iconic African game species. Bulls are powerfully built with a dark gray-blue coat, distinctive black stripes, and a flowing black mane. Their curved horns can reach 30 inches. Wildebeest are vigilant and fast, making them a challenging target. A mature bull in full condition is a magnificent trophy that embodies the spirit of the African plains.

Springbok

South Africa's national animal, the springbok is a medium-sized antelope renowned for its incredible speed and distinctive "pronking" display. Rams have elegant lyre-shaped horns reaching 12-16 inches. Their striking coloration — white face, dark eye stripe, and rich tan coat with a dark flank stripe — makes them a beautiful trophy. Springbok are alert and fast, offering exciting hunting opportunities across open plains.

Gemsbok

The gemsbok (Oryx gazella) is a striking antelope built for the harshest conditions. Both sexes carry formidable rapier-like horns that can reach 47 inches — among the longest of any antelope. Their distinctive black-and-white facial mask and gray-brown body make them instantly recognizable. Known for their toughness and adaptability, gemsbok offer a challenging hunt in open, arid terrain. A gemsbok trophy is a statement piece.

Burchell's Zebra

The Burchell's zebra is one of Africa's most iconic animals, instantly recognizable by its unique black and white stripe pattern. No two zebras have the same stripe arrangement. Stallions stand about 1.3 meters at the shoulder. Zebra hunting requires patience and precision — their eyesight is exceptional and they are constantly vigilant. A well-prepared zebra hide makes for one of the most striking trophies in any collection.

Sable Antelope

The sable antelope is widely considered the most beautiful of all African antelope. Mature bulls develop a striking jet-black coat with a white belly and facial markings, contrasting with their massive scimitar-shaped horns that curve backward and can exceed 60 inches. Sable are regal, powerful, and fiercely protective of their herd. Hunting sable is the ultimate challenge — they are alert, intelligent, and inhabit dense woodland. A mature sable bull is the crown jewel of African trophies.
